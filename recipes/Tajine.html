Ingredients:

  • Chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, or breast)
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Spices (paprika, cumin, cayenne pepper, ginger, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, and ras el hanout)
  • Preserved lemon
  • Olives
  • Water or chicken broth
  • Vegetables (potatoes, carrots, squash, green peas, cauliflower, green peppers, and zucchini)
  • Dried fruits (apricots, raisins, figs, and prunes)
  • Nuts (almonds or slivered almonds)

Moroccan chicken tagine is a delicious dish that consists of chicken, vegetables, dried fruits, olives, preserved lemon, and a variety of spices cooked slowly in a clay pot or a heavy-bottomed pot with a lid. The specific ingredients and their quantities may vary depending on the recipe and personal preferences. Some recipes may also include chickpeas, harissa, parsley, or cilantro. The slow-cooking process allows the chicken to become tender and the sauce to thicken, while the preserved lemon and olives add tanginess and saltiness to the dish. The result is a flavorful and satisfying meal that can be served with bread, couscous, or rice. Moroccan chicken tagine is not only a dish, but also an experience that showcases the richness and diversity of Moroccan cuisine.

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a tagine or a heavy-bottomed pot with a lid over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped garlic and onion, and sauté until softened and fragrant.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides.
  4. Add the spices (paprika, cumin, cayenne pepper, ginger, coriander, cinnamon, turmeric, and ras el hanout) and stir to coat the chicken.
  5. Add water or chicken broth to the pot, enough to cover the chicken halfway.
  6. Add the vegetables (potatoes, carrots, squash, green peas, cauliflower, green peppers, and zucchini) and dried fruits (apricots, raisins, figs, and prunes) to the pot.
  7. Cover the pot with a lid and simmer the chicken and vegetables over low heat for about 1 hour or until the chicken is tender and the sauce is thickened.
  8. Add the preserved lemon and olives to the pot, and continue to simmer for another 10-15 minutes.
  9. In a separate pan, toast the almonds until golden brown.
  10. Drizzle honey over the chicken and vegetables, and sprinkle the toasted almonds on top.
  11. Serve the Moroccan chicken tagine hot with bread, couscous, or rice.
